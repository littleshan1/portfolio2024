- Kyoto -

Kinkaku-ji ｜ 金閣寺（きんかくじ）

Kinkaku-ji, or the Golden Pavilion, is one of Kyoto’s most famous temples, known for its stunning gold-leaf exterior that reflects beautifully in the surrounding pond. Originally built as a shogun’s retirement villa, it is now a Zen temple and a symbol of Kyoto’s serene beauty.

kiyomizu dera ｜ 清水寺（きよみずでら）

Kiyomizu-dera is a historic Buddhist temple in Kyoto, famous for its large wooden stage that offers stunning views of the city and surrounding nature. The temple is dedicated to the deity Kannon and is particularly popular during cherry blossom and autumn leaf seasons. Its name, meaning "Pure Water Temple," comes from the Otawa Waterfall located within the grounds.

Fushimi Inari Taisha ｜ 伏見稲荷大社（ふしみいなりたいしゃ）

Fushimi Inari Taisha is a famous Shinto shrine in Kyoto, known for its thousands of vibrant red torii gates that lead up the mountain. Dedicated to Inari, the god of rice and agriculture, the shrine is a spiritual and cultural landmark. Visitors can hike through the gates, enjoy the peaceful atmosphere, and explore the shrine’s various buildings along the way.
