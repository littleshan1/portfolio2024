- Tokyo -

Shinjuku ｜ 新宿（しんじゅく）

Shinjuku is a lively district in Tokyo, known for its skyscrapers, shopping malls, and vibrant nightlife. It offers a mix of entertainment, dining, and relaxation, with spots like Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden for a peaceful escape. The area is also home to one of the busiest train stations, Shinjuku Station, connecting you to all of Tokyo.

1. Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden – a serene, spacious garden with seasonal blooms.

2. Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building – free observation decks with panoramic city views.

Shibuya ｜ 渋谷（しぶやく）

Shibuya is one of Tokyo's most famous districts, known for its vibrant youth culture, fashion, and entertainment. It’s a bustling hub filled with shops, restaurants, and iconic landmarks. Shibuya Crossing, the world-famous scramble intersection, is a must-see, showcasing Tokyo’s energy and fast pace.

1. Shibuya Sky – a rooftop observation deck with stunning city views.

2. Hachiko Statue – a popular meeting spot and tribute to Japan’s loyal dog.

Fuji Mountain ｜ 富士山（ふじさん）

Mount Fuji, Japan's tallest and most iconic mountain, is a majestic, often snow-capped volcano that draws millions of visitors each year. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it holds great cultural and spiritual significance in Japan. The mountain offers scenic beauty, hiking opportunities, and stunning views, especially in the summer when the climbing trails are open.

1. Fuji Five Lakes – a picturesque area with lakes offering views of Mount Fuji, boating, and hot springs.

2. Chureito Pagoda – a famous photo spot with the pagoda and Mount Fuji in the background.
